What is neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity, also known as neural plasticity, or brain plasticity, is the ability of neural networks in the brain to change through growth and reorganization. This refers to the brain's capacity to reorganize its structure, functions, and connections in response to learning, experience, and environmental factors.

How understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Neuroplasticity principles are very useful in the field of rehabilitation because it helps individuals recover from brain injuries, strokes and neurological conditions and this facilitates to recover lost functions.

Neuroplasticity help to recognise that the brain is not fixed but can change and grow encourages people to take on new challenges and learn new skills at any age. this include development of cognitive recovering techniques to improve memory, attention and problem-solving.

Neuroplacity principles also help in recognizing that continuing learning, cognitive engagement and social interaction can help improve cognitive function as people age.

Mindset: Is a habitual or characteristic mental attitude that determines how you will interpret and respond to situation.

Growth mindset

Growth mindset describes a way of viewing challenges and setbacks. People who have a growth mindset believe that even if they struggle with certain skills, their abilities aren't set in stone. They think that with work, their skills can improve over time.
